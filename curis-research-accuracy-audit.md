## PITCH DECK NUMBERS — Accuracy Analysis

### Slide 1 (Problem): "20,000 patients across network"

**Verdict: UNVERIFIED — plausible but aggressive**

- No public patient count data exists for Curis
- Industry benchmark: chiropractic practices typically see 100-300 active patients/month per location
- At 60+ locations x ~250 average = 15,000 active patients (conservative)
- At 60+ locations x ~350 average = 21,000 active patients (optimistic)
- 20,000 falls in the plausible range but **cannot be verified**
- **Risk**: If Curis has fewer active locations than claimed (some may be acquired but not fully operational), actual patient count could be lower

### Slide 1 (Problem): "70% need movement programming"

**Verdict: UNVERIFIED — reasonable industry estimate**

- No Curis-specific data available
- Given Curis treats chronic pain, musculoskeletal issues, and stress/mental health, 70% needing movement is defensible
- But this is an assumption, not a Curis-verified number
- **Recommendation**: Soften to "most" or cite a general industry study

### Slide 1 (Problem): "$5M lost annually in unrealized revenue"

**Verdict: DERIVED — math is loose**

- Appears to be: 20,000 patients x some per-patient revenue figure
- If it's 14,000 x $30/mo x 12 = $5.04M — the math works
- But the base assumptions (20K patients, 70% adoption) are themselves unverified
- **This is an aspirational/projected number, not a factual claim**

### Slide 2 (Gap): "$3-4M/year to hire 50+ coaches"

**Verdict: MATHEMATICALLY SOUND**

- BLS median personal trainer salary: $46,180
- With benefits/overhead (30-40%): ~$60K-$65K per coach
- 50 coaches x $60-65K = $3M-$3.25M
- For clinical fitness specialists (healthcare setting): $62-82K + benefits = $80-107K
- 50 x $80-107K = $4M-$5.3M
- **$3-4M is a reasonable range** for 50+ fitness professionals in healthcare settings

### Slide 5 (Business Case): "$10/mo charge, $5/mo cost, $5/mo profit"

**Verdict: MOVRA'S OWN PRICING — internally consistent**

- This is Movra's pricing model, not a Curis claim
- The $10/mo patient charge and $5/mo platform cost are Movra's business decisions
- Whether patients will pay $10/mo for a movement app is a market assumption
- **These are projections, not verified facts**

### Slide 5 (Business Case): "14K patients x $5/mo = $840K/yr"

**Verdict: MATH CORRECT, BASE ASSUMPTION UNVERIFIED**

- 14,000 x $5 x 12 = $840,000 — arithmetic is correct
- 14K appears to be 70% of 20,000 (patients who "need movement programming")
- Both the 20K base and 70% conversion are unverified
- **Additionally**: Assuming 100% of "needing" patients actually enroll at $10/mo is very aggressive. Typical SaaS conversion rates are 2-10% for healthcare apps
- **If even 50% of the 14K enroll**: $420K/yr
- **If 25% enroll**: $210K/yr

### Slide 6 (ROI): "$8.8M total first-year value"

**Verdict: HIGHLY SPECULATIVE**

Breakdown:
- $840K direct profit — dependent on 14K patients at 100% conversion (aggressive)
- $4M retained revenue — completely unverifiable assumption about patient retention value
- $4M recovered capacity — completely unverifiable assumption about freed-up resources
- **The $840K is at least derivable from stated assumptions. The $4M + $4M "soft" ROI figures are not defensible with data.**

### Slide 6 (ROI): "$900K investment"

**Verdict: UNCLEAR DERIVATION**

- With the current pricing tiers: Solo $97/mo, Single Clinic $297/mo, Enterprise $1K-$10K/mo
- For 60+ locations: If average ~$1.25K/mo per location -> 60 x $1.25K x 12 = $900K/yr
- Or: a single enterprise contract at $75K/mo x 12 = $900K
- **Either way, this is Curis's cost to adopt Movra — the derivation should be explicit**

### Slide 6 (ROI): "~10x ROI"

**Verdict: MATHEMATICALLY CORRECT but based on speculative inputs**

- $8.8M / $900K = 9.78x ~ 10x
- The math is right, but garbage-in-garbage-out applies
- The "hard" ROI (direct profit only): $840K / $900K = 0.93x — **less than 1x ROI**
- **This means at the "hard numbers" level, Curis would lose money in Year 1 unless the $4M + $4M soft benefits are real**

### CTA Slide: "$3-5M in unrealized annual revenue"

**Verdict: INCONSISTENT**

- Slide 1 says "$5M lost annually"
- CTA slide says "$3-5M"
- These should be consistent throughout the deck

## RED FLAGS & RECOMMENDATIONS

### Critical Issues

1. **The "hard" ROI is negative.** At $840K profit vs $900K investment, Curis would lose $60K in Year 1 on direct numbers alone. The 10x ROI depends entirely on soft/indirect benefits ($4M retained revenue + $4M recovered capacity) that are unsubstantiated. **This is the most dangerous number in the deck.**

2. **14K patients at 100% adoption is unrealistic.** Even if Curis has 14K patients who need movement, getting all of them to pay $10/mo for an app requires extraordinary adoption rates. Healthcare app adoption is typically 5-20%.

3. **Location count is murky.** Curis claims "50+" and "60+" but the website only shows ~10 locations. The real number is probably somewhere in the 25-60 range when including Curis Express/Walmart locations. The 500+ Walmart potential is theoretical and distant.

4. **Patient count is pure assumption.** 20,000 is in a plausible range but cannot be fact-checked. Getting this wrong by even 50% cascades through every financial projection.

### Moderate Issues

5. **The $3-4M hiring cost comparison** is the strongest number in the deck — it's based on real salary data and is independently verifiable. Keep it.

6. **Dossier claims "three consecutive years" on Inc. 5000** — verified for at least 2 (2023, 2024). The 2022 claim (#142) is stated in the 2023 PR as "second consecutive year," confirming 2022 as well. So "three consecutive" starting 2022 is correct.

7. **"PE backing"** is implied by board expertise language but no specific PE firm is named. Don't overstate this.

### Suggestions

- **Reframe the ROI**: Lead with the $840K direct profit, then present retained revenue and recovered capacity as "additional estimated value" rather than hard numbers
- **Use adoption tiers**: Show scenarios — 25% adoption, 50% adoption, 100% adoption — to be credible
- **Verify patient count with Austin**: Ask him if he has any intel on Curis's actual patient volume
- **Make the $5M consistent**: Pick either "$5M" or "$3-5M" and use it throughout
- **Consider adjusting the investment figure**: At $900K cost and $840K direct return, the pitch loses credibility. Either lower the assumed cost (smaller initial deployment) or increase the patient revenue assumptions with a phased rollout
